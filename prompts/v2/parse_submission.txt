## Technique Vocabulary & Definitions

When extracting `training_techniques`, classify each technique into one of the categories below. Each entry is a dictionary with `"category"` and `"data"` (a category-specific key-value dict). Only include techniques that are explicitly mentioned or strongly implied.

**IMPORTANT**: The `category` field must be exactly one of the 12 values listed below. Do not invent new category names.

### 1. `quantization`
Reducing the numerical precision of model weights to save artifact size.
- **data**: `method` (str: e.g., "int8", "int6", "int5", "fp16", "mixed int6/int8", "QAT", "STE QAT", "GPTQ", "GPTQ-lite", "late QAT", "bitsandbytes"), `bits` (int or null: e.g., 6, 8), `scope` (str or null: which parts — e.g., "all", "MLP", "embeddings", "block weights")
- **Includes**: QAT, STE QAT, GPTQ, GPTQ-lite, late QAT, bitsandbytes, post-training quantization, mixed precision quantization
- **Does NOT include**: zstd/zlib/gzip (use `compression`), magnitude pruning (use `regularization`)

### 2. `architecture_modification`
Structural changes to the model architecture beyond a standard Transformer.
- **data**: `component` (str: e.g., "XSA", "SmearGate", "BigramHash", "TrigramHash", "RoPE", "Partial RoPE", "MLP3x", "depth recurrence", "weight tying", "KV head count", "Gated Attention", "Value Residual", "VE128"), `description` (str: brief explanation of the modification), `parameters` (dict or null: any relevant hyperparameters like `{"layers": 4}`, `{"dimensions": 16}`)
- **Includes**: weight tying, depth recurrence, U-Net skip connections, XSA, BigramHash, TrigramHash, Gated Attention, Value Residual, VE128, MLP activation choices (ReLU², LeakyReLU), attention modifications
- **Does NOT include**: SWA/EMA (use `weight_averaging`), OrthoInit (use `initialization`), zstd (use `compression`), GPTQ/GPTQ-lite (use `quantization`)

### 3. `optimizer_technique`
Optimizer choice and associated tuning beyond standard Adam/AdamW.
- **data**: `method` (str: e.g., "Muon", "Parallel Muon", "Adam", "AdamW", "SGD", "LAMB", "NorMuon"), `weight_decay` (float or null), `momentum` (float or null), `other_params` (dict or null: any other relevant optimizer parameters)

### 4. `weight_averaging`
Post-training or during-training weight averaging schemes.
- **data**: `method` (str: e.g., "SWA", "EMA", "Polyak averaging", "Tight SWA", "EMA + SWA"), `parameters` (dict or null: e.g., `{"decay": 0.4}`, `{"start_step": 120}`)
- **Includes**: SWA, EMA, Polyak averaging, Tight SWA. SWA is ALWAYS `weight_averaging`, never `architecture_modification`.

### 5. `compression`
Compression of the final artifact (model + code) to fit within the 16MB limit.
- **data**: `method` (str: e.g., "zstd", "zlib", "gzip", "lzma", "custom"), `level` (int or null: compression level if specified)
- **Includes**: zstd, zlib, gzip, lzma, any artifact compression. zstd is ALWAYS `compression`, never `quantization`.

### 6. `evaluation_technique`
Techniques applied during evaluation/inference to improve val_bpb.
- **data**: `method` (str: e.g., "sliding window eval", "long context eval", "stride-based eval"), `parameters` (dict or null: e.g., `{"stride": 64}`, `{"context_length": 4096}`)

### 7. `test_time_training`
Adapting the model at test time on already-evaluated tokens (TTT).
- **data**: `method` (str: e.g., "LoRA TTT", "full TTT", "score-first TTT"), `parameters` (dict or null: e.g., `{"rank": 8}`, `{"learning_rate": 0.001}`)

### 8. `initialization`
Non-standard weight initialization strategies.
- **data**: `method` (str: e.g., "OrthoInit", "spectral init", "resid mix", "OvertoneInit", "phase-transition resid_mix"), `description` (str or null: brief explanation)
- **Includes**: OrthoInit, spectral init, resid mix, OvertoneInit. OrthoInit is ALWAYS `initialization`, never `architecture_modification`.

### 9. `sequence_length`
Training or evaluation at non-default sequence lengths.
- **data**: `train_length` (int or null), `eval_length` (int or null)

### 10. `lr_schedule`
Learning rate scheduling strategies.
- **data**: `method` (str: e.g., "warmdown", "cosine decay", "linear warmup", "warmdown3500"), `parameters` (dict or null: e.g., `{"warmdown_steps": 3500}`)

### 11. `regularization`
Regularization techniques beyond standard dropout.
- **data**: `method` (str: e.g., "dropout", "weight decay", "label smoothing", "layerwise LN scale", "magnitude pruning", "logit softcap"), `parameters` (dict or null)
- **Includes**: dropout, weight decay, label smoothing, magnitude pruning, structured pruning, logit softcap, LN scale

### 12. `other`
Any other notable technique not covered above. **Use sparingly** — before placing a technique here, verify it does not fit into any of the 11 categories above. In particular:
- Pruning / magnitude pruning → `regularization`
- QAT variants / late QAT → `quantization`
- zstd / zlib / artifact compression → `compression`
- SWA / EMA → `weight_averaging`
- OrthoInit → `initialization`
- **data**: `description` (str: what the technique does), `parameters` (dict or null)

## Instructions
Extract the following fields as a JSON object:

- **pr_number**: (integer) The PR number
- **title**: (string) The PR title
- **author**: (string) The GitHub username of the author
- **status**: (string) One of: "merged", "open", "closed", "disqualified". Infer from state/labels.
- **is_record**: (boolean) Whether this submission set a new record at the time. Look for labels or mentions of "record" or "best".
- **val_bpb**: (float or null) The validation bits-per-byte score reported. Look for numbers like "1.234 bpb" or "val bpb: 1.234".
- **architecture**: (string or null) The base model architecture family. Use a short canonical name: "Transformer", "LSTM", "RNN", "Mamba", "GPT-2", "Hybrid". Do NOT include layer count, dimension, head count, or other config details — those belong in training_techniques entries.
- **quantization**: (string or null) Concise quantization summary, max ~20 characters. Examples: "int8 QAT", "mixed int6/int8", "GPTQ 6-bit", "int4 RTN". Do NOT write full sentences or include compression details.
- **optimizer**: (string or null) Primary optimizer used (e.g., "Adam", "AdamW", "Muon", "Parallel Muon", "NorMuon")
- **training_techniques**: (array of objects) Each object has:
  - `"category"`: one of the 12 category names above (e.g., "quantization", "architecture_modification", "weight_averaging", etc.)
  - `"data"`: a dictionary with the category-specific fields defined above
- **compression**: (string or null) Compression method for the artifact (e.g., "zstd-22", "zlib", "gzip")
- **novel_contributions**: (array of strings) Key novel ideas or contributions described in this submission.
- **artifact_size**: (string or null) Total artifact size if mentioned (e.g., "10MB", "256KB")

Respond with ONLY the JSON object, no other text.
